## Privacy narrative

Always-listening and always-seeing wearables are different from phones because they move sensing into ordinary social interaction. The main privacy issue is not just ÔÇťmy data,ÔÇŁ but ÔÇťeveryone around me becomes input.ÔÇŁ Recent work on camera glasses finds real tension between wearer benefits and bystander expectations, while classic HCI work on always-on wearable cameras and newer work on speech recording show that ambiguity around recording state directly harms social acceptability. ţłÇciteţłéturn11search0ţłéturn12search0ţłéturn12search17ţłéturn25search0ţłü

Local inference changes that risk profile in three ways. First, it reduces the need to upload raw audio and images. Second, it improves latency and reliability for moments where a wearable should feel immediate. Third, it reduces service fragility: cloud memory wearables can lose regions, features, or entire products, and HumaneÔÇÖs shutdown is the clearest reminder that a server-dependent wearable can stop being useful overnight. By contrast, the major platform owners are now explicitly shipping on-device model access precisely because privacy, latency, and offline use are first-class requirements. ţłÇciteţłéturn10search0ţłéturn34view1ţłéturn36search9ţłéturn34view3ţłéturn16view2ţłéturn23view4ţłéturn30search3ţłü
